Regulatory Framework:
Wema Bank operates under national regulations (e.g., Central Bank guidance) and international standards for anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), and sanctions compliance. The bank implements policies to detect and disrupt illicit financial flows.

Core Components:
1. KYC & Customer Due Diligence:
- Verify identity and beneficial ownership for all customers; enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers such as PEPs.
- Ongoing monitoring to reassess risk profiles over time.

2. Transaction Monitoring:
- Automated systems profile transaction behaviors and flag patterns that indicate structuring, layering, or suspicious movement.
- Alerts are investigated by compliance teams; confirmed suspicious activity reports (SARs) are filed with regulators where required.

3. Sanctions Screening:
- Screening customers and transactions against global sanctions lists to ensure no prohibited parties or flows are processed.
- Reject or freeze transactions involving sanctioned entities and notify authorities per legal requirements.

4. Reporting & Recordkeeping:
- Suspicious transaction reporting, regulatory filings, and maintaining records to support audits and investigations.
- Cooperate with law enforcement and regulatory inquiries while protecting customer confidentiality to the extent permitted by law.

5. Training & Culture:
- Regular staff training on AML controls, identifying red flags, and escalation procedures.
- Strong governance with a dedicated compliance function, independent testing, and board oversight.

Penalties & Enforcement:
- Non-compliance carries regulatory penalties, reputational damage, and legal consequences. The bank runs internal audits and remedial programs to close control gaps.
