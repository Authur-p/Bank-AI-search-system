 Introduction
Wema Bank PLC is committed to the highest possible standards of transparency and accountability in all her business. The Bank is determined to maintain a culture of honesty and opposition to fraud and corruption
The Bank will not tolerate fraud, theft or dishonesty. AII reported incidents, including internal fraud will be thoroughly investigated with the utmost objectivity and confidentiality. The Bank will take firm and vigorous actions against any individual or group who has committed a fraud. Such action may involve disciplinary action, civil and/or criminal legal proceedings. Disciplinary proceedings may result in the dismissal of an employee. The bank will maintain an effective fraud control environment to ensure fraud prevention and detection.
The Wema Bank Fraud Management Policy outlined employees’ expected behavior, how to report a suspected fraud, the role of various departments/business units within the Bank‹ and the Bank’s commitment to increasing employees' awareness, training and recording of possible fraud.
The Fraud Management Policy should be read in conjunction with Anti-bribery and Corruption Policy, Enterprise Risk Management Policy, Operational Risk Management Policy. Human Capital Management Policy and Whistle Blowing Policy of the Bank.
Scope
This policy applies to any fraud, irregularity, or suspected irregularity involving employees as well as shareholders, consultants, vendors, contractors and/or any other parties with a business relationship with Wema Bank PLC including outsider who had committed fraud on Wema Bank PLC

Purpose
Wema Bank PLC does not condone fraudulent behavior. This policy covers guidelines and responsibilities regarding appropriate actions that must be followed to increase the awareness of the prevention and the investigation of fraud. The fundamental purpose of this policy is to ensure that each business within Wema Bank PLC has the element in place that are necessary to prevent and detect fraud that can originate internally and externally.
The underlying threat of fraud is a cost of doing business within the financial services industry Individuals and organized crime groups have historically targeted financial institutions to obtain account information and funds by exploiting vulnerabilities through a variety of fraud schemes.
Fraud occurs from two sources, internal and external with occasions of collusion. This policy applies to any fraud, irregularity or suspected Irregularity, involving employees and/or any other parties in a business relation5hlp with Wema Bank PLC. Regardless of the fraud scheme, financial institutions fate a significant problem and challenge in managing the fraud threat as well as applying resources needed to counter the threat.
The Bank is committed Fa protecting her revenue, expenditure and assets or those of its customers from any attempt by members of the public, contractors, agents, intermediaries or its own employees to gain financial or other benefits by deceit or dishonest conduct.
The result of not addressing fraud risk at a consistent and effective level is financial loss, lo3s of reputation and trust within the marketplace, as well as potentially violating Specific regulatory controls.
The policy is designed to protect public money and assets and to protect the integrity, security and reputation of the Bank, her employees and the services it provides to the general public. The future, health and reputation of Wema Bank Plc are dependent. in part, on how well it manages the threat of fraud. The usage of this policy is in conjunction with Information Security Policy, Whistle Blowing Policy and Human Capital Management Policy of the Bank.

How do we prevent Fraud?
A system of internal control has been implemented to prevent fraud from occurring. Key fraud prevention controls include:
• Upholding the standards of behavior detailed within the HCM PoI1cy Manual- The Manual provides a set of guiding principles to help our people make decisions in their day to day work to ensure that they maintain high ethical standards
• Compliance with the law and Wema Bank Plc.’s polices and procedures, Wema Bank PLC has designed systems and processes that comply with the law and provides relevant policies and training to help our people achieve this.
• Wema Bank PLC Risk management process. Wema Bank PLC has adopted the Enterprise and Operational Risk management process as part of normal business practices. To prevent fraud from occurring, the Bank identifies and evaluates internal and external fraud threats and implements preventative controls that mitigate or minimize significant fraud risks.
• Fraud Vulnerably Assessment. At regular intervals, Wema Bank PLC undertakes an organization-wide risk assessment of its operational and strategic fraud risks which provides clarity and understanding around the full spectrum of fraud risks,
• Background check on staff from Central Bank of Nigeria and Law Enforcement Agency before confirmation of their employment
• Confirmation of Certificates presented by the new employee to the Bank before confirmation of employment
• Managers and Supervisors employ sound fraud risk management practices within all activities for which they are responsible and ensure the effective operations of the following preventive controls:
• Adequate separation of duties (more than one employee is Involved in key tasks)
• proper authorization procedures transactions must be recommended and
• physical security over the bank’s assets and
• independent monitoring and checking of data and documentation (checks and balances).
• Administrative Controls formalize standards, rules, procedures and control discipline to ensure that the organization’s general and application controls are properly governed, executed and enforced. These are enshrined in frameworks. Wema bank use3 the Control Objectives for Information and Related Technology version 5 (COBIT 5) framework for Information systems control governance
• Application Input Controls. These controls check data for accuracy and completeness when they log into the system. There are specific input controls for input authorization, data conversion, data editing and error handling
• Application Processing Controls. These controls establish that data are complete and accurate during processing and updating.
• Application Output Controls. These controls ensure that the results of computer processing are accurate, complete, and properly distributed.
• Network Controls. These are controls such as Firewalls which help prevent unauthorized access to the banks' corporate network.
• Data Security Controls ensure critical business data are not subjected to unauthorized access, change Dr destruction while they are in use or In storage.
• Ensuring complexity of passwords used by staff to access the Bank’s network and other Applications
• Disabling or staff access ro the Bank's network during vacation or leave of absence.
• Network segmentation to prevent unauthorized access
• Review of system logs to identify anomaly and to guard against such once discovered.
How Is Fraud Detected?
A system of internal control has been implemented to detect fraud as soon as possible after it has occurred. Key fraud detection controls include:
• Inappropriate approval and authorization processes.
• independent reconciliations
• physical checks /stock-taking
• Providing fal5e or misleading information or failing to provide information when there is an obligation to do so
• whistle blowing
• analysis of management accounting reports
• Network Intrusion detection solution For detecting unauthorized access to the bank’s corporate network
• File Integrity monitoring Solution for detecting Unauthorized changes to critical system configuration files.
• Database Activity Monitoring Solution for detecting unauthorized activities at the back-end or database level
Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) which performs real-time analysis of security alert5 and audit logs generated by application and servers to detect unauthorized activities
On a regular basis, Internal Audit Department will perform forensic data analysis, unannounced audit‹ end a rolling program of audits focusing on the effectiveness of fraud prevention and detection controls.
Investigation

An investigation of fraudulent activity or irregularity will be undertaken by appropriately qualified personnel in the Internal Audit Department depending on the matter and the content of the disclosure.
The aim of the investigation of the fraudulent activity is to Substantiate or refute the claims, identify the root cause of an event, make recommendations to management on ways to prevent re-occurrence and appropriate sanction of any erring employee.
There are some times where a customer has complained of fraud on his/her account and there is exigency to refund a complainant before the conclusion of the investigation, Chief Audit Executive will obtain an exceptional approval from Management to refund the ‹complainant pending the conclusion of the investigation. This will usually be done where preliminary review has shown that the affected customer is not involved in the conformed fraud.
Upon the approval, ir the complainant is genuinely due for the refund, the appropriate General Ledger (Fraud & Forgery) account will be debited and affected customer'S account will be credited.
If a staff benefited from the fraud, the fraudulent staff will be arrested after interrogation for possible recovery and/or prosecution. Chief Audit Executive will file and process the claim from the insurers through the Brokers of the Bank on Fidelity Insurance Policy after submitting the Internal investigation report and documents evidencing the fraud,
Insurance company will appoint a Loss Adjuster who will review the case and conduct an investigation to confirm if the amount claimed Is due for Indemnity or otherwise Where the fraud is due to control lapses, a penal charge is applied and the balance thereof less Policy excess charge is advised Fa the Bank via Discharge Voucher which will be executed by the representative of the Bank.
Where the fraud is committed by a third party against the bank or her customer, the address of the third party who benefitted from the proceeds of fraud will be obtained from his/her Bank via a Baskets Order through the instrumentality of the Pa\ice. The Police will thereafter visit the address to apprehend the suspect for interrogation, recovery, and prosecution by NIBSS if he/she is a customer of the bank, otherwise, his/her BVN Shall be Watch Listed by NIBSS through the request of be Chief Audit Executive of his/her Bank.
In respective of the individual involved in the fraud, Head, Security Desk in Internal Audit Department will follow up with the Police and the Court to ensure judgement is delivered on the case at a Court of competent jurisdiction.
Staff who had been discovered to be involved in the fraud and/or negligent in the course of duty which had allowed the fraud will be summoned to face the panel of Disciplinary Committee of the Bank to explain his/her role on the incident. The Committee is headed by one of the Executive Directors or its Designate with the Chief Human Resources Officer, Chief Audit Executive, Chief Compliance Officer and Plead, Operations as members of the Committee while Heads of Unions and other relevant officers of the Bank are observers. The decision of the Disciplinary Committee will be presented to the Executive Committee for ratification before it becomes binding on the staff.
The Bank usually dismiss the staff who has been established to be involved in a fraud from her employment, The bank will notify the Secretary, Banking Supervision, Central Bank of Nigeria and Executive Director, Operations. Nigeria Deposit Insurance Corporation of the termination of the staff during the monthly returns in compliance with the Bankers’ Committee decision of J41 ’ December, 1982 and the provisions of sections 39 and 40 of NDIC Decree No. 22 of 1988 to avert recycling of such staff In the financial industry.
Where the individual involved is a staff, upon judgement, the decision of the Court is circularized to all staff of he Bank to sensitize them of the incident and to serve as a deterrent to other staff who might want to be involved in such an act.
